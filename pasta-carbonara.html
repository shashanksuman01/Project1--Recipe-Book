Classic Pasta Carbonara

A traditional Italian pasta dish with eggs, cheese, and pancetta

25 minutes
Serves 4
Easy
Back to Main Courses

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti or linguine
  • 200g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs plus 1 egg yolk
  • 100g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • 50g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt for pasta water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook pasta according to package directions until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water before draining.
  2. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add pancetta and cook until crispy and golden, about 5-6 minutes.
  3. Add minced garlic to the pancetta and cook for another minute until fragrant. Remove from heat.
  4. In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, egg yolk, both cheeses, and a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper.
  5. Add the hot, drained pasta to the skillet with pancetta. Toss to combine and remove from heat.
  6. Quickly add the egg and cheese mixture to the pasta, tossing constantly to prevent the eggs from scrambling. Add pasta water gradually until you achieve a creamy consistency.
  7. Season with additional black pepper and salt if needed. The pasta should be creamy and well-coated.
  8. Serve immediately in warmed bowls, garnished with extra cheese, black pepper, and fresh parsley.

Chef's Tips

  • The key to perfect carbonara is working quickly and keeping the pasta hot when mixing with the egg mixture.
  • Never add cream to traditional carbonara - the creaminess comes from the eggs and cheese.
  • Use room temperature eggs for easier mixing and to prevent scrambling.
  • Save some pasta water - its starch content helps create the perfect sauce consistency.
