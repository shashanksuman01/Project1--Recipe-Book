Classic Caesar Salad

Crisp romaine lettuce with homemade dressing and croutons

15 minutes
Serves 4
Easy
Back to Appetizers

Ingredients

For the Salad:

  • 2 large heads romaine lettuce, chopped
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 2 cups homemade croutons
  • 4 anchovy fillets (optional)

For the Dressing:

  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 anchovy fillets, mashed
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

For the Croutons:

  • 4 slices day-old bread, cubed
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Make croutons first: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Toss bread cubes with olive oil, garlic, and salt. Bake for 10-15 minutes until golden and crispy.
  2. For the dressing: In a small bowl, mash garlic and anchovy fillets into a paste.
  3. Whisk in egg yolk, lemon juice, and Dijon mustard until well combined.
  4. Slowly drizzle in olive oil while whisking constantly to create an emulsion.
  5. Stir in grated Parmesan cheese and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Wash and thoroughly dry romaine lettuce. Chop into bite-sized pieces.
  7. In a large salad bowl, toss lettuce with enough dressing to coat evenly.
  8. Add half of the Parmesan cheese and toss again.
  9. Top with croutons, remaining Parmesan cheese, and anchovy fillets if using.
  10. Serve immediately with additional black pepper and lemon wedges.

Chef's Tips

  • Use the freshest romaine lettuce possible for the best crunch and flavor.
  • Make sure lettuce is completely dry to prevent watery dressing.
  • The raw egg yolk creates authentic Caesar dressing - use pasteurized eggs if concerned.
  • Homemade croutons make all the difference - avoid store-bought when possible.
